High mass star cluster formation

Adam Ginsburg

What is a high-mass cluster?

  • Gravitationally bound collection of stars that survives the loss of gas
  • Collection of coeval stars that 'fully samples' the IMF
  • Clusters where interactions are important?  
  • Around \(10^4~\mathrm{M}_\odot \), \(v_{esc} \gtrsim10\) km s\(^{-1}\), so ionization does not disrupt gas
Are z=0 high-mass clusters analogs to Globular Cluster progenitors?

Is star formation in high-mass clusters different?

  • The IMF should depend on density, feedback (e.g., Jones & Bate 2018)
  • Feedback from one star affects many in clustered regions (Rosen+ 2016, Ginsburg+ 2017)

Observations:

Forming high-mass clusters in the Galaxy

  • Galactic plane surveys find few (~10s) of high-mass protoclusters
    • Ginsburg+ 2012, Urquhart+ 2014a, b, 2018, Longmore+ 2014, Contreras+ 2017

Observations:

Massive clusters in other galaxies

  • Forming and very young: "Super Star Clusters"
    • e.g., Leroy+ 2018, Johnson+ 2018, Cohen+ 2018
  • Gas-free but O-star rich: "Young Massive Clusters"
    • M83 (Hollyhead+ 2015, Adamo+ 2015, ) Antennae (Cabrera-Ziri+ 2015)

Super star clusters in NGC 253

Leroy+ 2018

How do massive clusters get their mass?

Two possibilities:

  1. The mass is pre-assembled, then collapses
    • Basis of simplified models
  2. The mass is assembled as stars form: there is no starless phase
    • Better supported by observational evidence
  3. Stars form, then merge into clusters
    (e.g., Fujii+)

Simulation: Accretion from large scales

Smilgys & Bonnell 2017

Simulations of forming individual clusters
  • Limited mass resolution: no massive clusters with \(M_{max} > 10~\mathrm{M}_{\odot}\) resolve low-mass stars
    (e.g., Jim Dale+, Kim, Kim, & Ostriker)
    • focus on cloud-scale effects
  • Lower mass, higher resolution simulations
    (e.g., Rosen, Krumholz, Peters)
    • Feedback from massive stars affects SF outcomes
